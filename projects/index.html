  • Future of work and small businesses. In Google's internal incubator, Area 120, I researched and built technology that explored the topics of the gig economy, youth unemployment, and impacts of COVID-19 in both the US and abroad.

    • Stride, an online community for small businesses to give and get advice (2020).
    • Kormo, a marketplace for entry-level service sector jobs in Bangladesh and other parts of Asia (2019).
    • Ally, connecting African entrepeneurs with funding and mentorship (2018).

  • Offline files. I built technologies that help people, primarily in India and Sub-Saharan Africa to download files on Chrome (2017) and share them offline on Youtube Go and, eventually, Files Go (2016).

  • Paper-digital tools in rural health. I explored the persistence of paper and the practicality of digital tools in low-resource settings, designing and implementing hybrid tools on the paper-digital spectrum for rural health centers.

    • Beyond the partograph, designing and evaluating tools for childbirth in peripheral health centers in India (2015).
    • printr, designing and evaluating tangible user interfaces with visual and computational feedback for health and microfinance institutions in greater Accra, Ghana (2014).
    • Raxa JSS EMR, a health system for a rural hospital and its associated community health workers in India (2012).

  • Artificial intelligence and health information. I researched different means of collecting and analyzing health information through my undergraduate thesis in topic models and autism data from clinical settings and online medical forums (2015) and my assistance building tools for care plan management for children with complex conditions (2015).
