Worker visibility in frontline health

Frontline health workers like home health aides or community health workers serve as the backbone of their respective health systems but are vulnerable workers who face challenges due to their lack of clinical status and identities as predominantly women or people of color. I am investigating how technology could help them receive the support, respect, and recognition they deserve.

  • Interviewed FHWs in NYC and India about what invisible work they do and what they do about it.
  • Presented at workshops (CHI 2022, CSCW 2021) and submitted papers that are conditionally accepted.

Response bias in accessibility

Accessibility technology can really help reduce barriers for people with disabilities, but its history is filled with examples of well-intentioned but ultimately harmful technology. This is potentially a result of response bias, where participants of research studies may not answer in a way that represents what they actually think because they feel pressured by the power dynamic with the researcher or study design.

  • Interviewed researchers about manifestations and mitgations in their research (ASSETS 2021).
  • Started experiments to see if participants demonstrate the response bias researchers described.
